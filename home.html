Helmet and Extravehicular Visor Assembly

Helmets are made of clear, impact-resistant plastic, with interior padding for comfort.

Visors are covered with a thin layer of gold to help filter out harsh rays of sunlight.

The most important component is the velcro attached to the inside of the helmet, allowing astronauts to scratch their noses during long space walks.

Hard Upper Torso Assembly

The basic building block of the whole EMU, the Hard Upper Torso (HUT) assembly serves as a rigid base for the rest of the EMU to connect to.

Because a few important organs like the heart are in the upper torse, the HUT also provides some protection from micrometeroids.

Gloves

One of the trickiest components of the EMU suit, spacesuit gloves must walk the line between providing sufficient protection while still allowing astronauts to use thier fingers and execute tasks.

Gloves aid easy movement of the wrist, have rubberized fingertips for improved grip, and are flexible enough for astronauts to be able to bend their fingers and grasp and grip tools. EMU gloves are made of many layers of insulating materials for pressurization, warmth, and protection.

Liquid Cooling and Ventilation Garment

In a true fashion statement, astronauts wear long johns under thier EMUs, covering everything but their head, hands and feet. Tubes are woven into the fabric and pumped with cold water to keep the astronaut cool.

Primary Life Support Subsystem

Like ramen sustains you, the primary life support subsystem is the most important feature of the EMU, as it is what actually allows the astronaut to venture into space.

The subsystem delivers oxygen to the helmet for the freshest possible breathing air and pulls carbon dioxide breathed out from the extremities. The subsystem also supplies electricity powering the suit and includes the water tank providing cool water to the Liquid Cooling and Ventilation Gament.

SAFER (Simplified Aid for Extravehicular Activity Rescue)

SAFER is attached to the backpack and contains several small thruster jets that let an astronaut fly back to the space station if they get separated for some reason.
